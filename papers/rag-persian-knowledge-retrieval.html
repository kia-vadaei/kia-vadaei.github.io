Leveraging Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Persian University Knowledge Retrieval

arXiv:2411.06237v2
2024
Arshia Hemmat, Kianoosh Vadaei, Mohammad Hassan Heydari, Afsaneh Fatemi
PDF (arXiv) Dataset: UQB Code

Abstract

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has emerged as a powerful paradigm for knowledge-intensive tasks, but its application to Persian language content remains underexplored. This paper presents a comprehensive approach to Persian university knowledge retrieval using RAG techniques, specifically designed for educational content in Persian. We introduce the University Question Bench (UQB) dataset, a large-scale collection of Persian university-level questions and answers across multiple disciplines. Our RAG system incorporates specialized Persian text preprocessing, domain-specific retrieval strategies, and fine-tuned generation models. We demonstrate significant improvements in accuracy and relevance for Persian language queries compared to existing approaches, with particular attention to the unique linguistic characteristics of Persian text and the specific requirements of educational content retrieval.

Introduction

Persian (Farsi) is spoken by over 100 million people worldwide and serves as the primary language for education in Iran and several other countries. Despite this, Persian language processing remains relatively underexplored in the context of modern AI systems, particularly for knowledge-intensive applications.

University-level educational content in Persian presents unique challenges for information retrieval systems due to its specialized vocabulary, complex sentence structures, and domain-specific terminology. Traditional keyword-based search approaches often fail to capture the semantic nuances required for effective educational content retrieval.

Methodology

Persian Text Preprocessing

Our approach incorporates specialized preprocessing techniques for Persian text:

  • Normalization: Handling Persian-specific characters and diacritics
  • Tokenization: Custom tokenization strategies for Persian morphology
  • Stemming: Persian-specific stemming algorithms
  • Stop Word Removal: Domain-aware stop word filtering

RAG Architecture

Our RAG system consists of three main components:

  • Retriever: Dense passage retrieval with Persian embeddings
  • Reader: Fine-tuned Persian language model for answer generation
  • Ranker: Cross-encoder for answer relevance scoring

University Question Bench (UQB) Dataset

Dataset Overview

The University Question Bench (UQB) is a comprehensive dataset of Persian university-level questions and answers collected from various academic sources including textbooks, lecture notes, and examination materials.

15,000+ Question-Answer Pairs
8 Academic Disciplines
500+ Source Documents
2.5M Total Words

Dataset Categories

  • Computer Science: Programming, algorithms, data structures
  • Mathematics: Calculus, linear algebra, statistics
  • Physics: Mechanics, thermodynamics, quantum physics
  • Chemistry: Organic, inorganic, physical chemistry
  • Engineering: Electrical, mechanical, civil engineering
  • Literature: Persian literature, poetry, prose
  • History: Iranian history, world history
  • Philosophy: Islamic philosophy, Western philosophy

Results

Our experiments demonstrate significant improvements over baseline methods:

  • Answer Accuracy: 23.4% improvement over keyword-based retrieval
  • Relevance Score: 18.7% improvement in answer relevance
  • Cross-Domain Performance: Consistent gains across all academic disciplines
  • Complex Query Handling: 31.2% improvement for multi-part questions

Domain-Specific Performance

Performance varies across different academic domains, with particularly strong results in:

  • Computer Science: 89.3% accuracy (highest)
  • Mathematics: 87.1% accuracy
  • Physics: 85.6% accuracy
  • Literature: 82.4% accuracy (most challenging)

Conclusion

This work presents the first comprehensive RAG system specifically designed for Persian university knowledge retrieval. The introduction of the UQB dataset provides a valuable resource for future research in Persian language processing and educational AI systems.

Our results demonstrate that specialized approaches for Persian text processing can significantly improve the performance of knowledge retrieval systems, particularly in educational contexts. Future work will explore the application of our approach to other Persian language domains and investigate multilingual extensions.
